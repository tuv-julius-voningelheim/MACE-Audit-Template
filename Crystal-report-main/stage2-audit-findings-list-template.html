Stage 2 Audit Finding List (Confidential) – Angepasst an TÜV SÜD Vorlage ID 2413 Rev22 + Interview-Feedback

Dieses HTML zeigt das Ziel-Design für die Audit Finding List. Angepasst an die offizielle TÜV SÜD Vorlage (ID 2413, Rev22) und Interview-Feedback von Sebastian Reuter. Alle Schemes (ISO 13485, MDR, MDSAP) müssen pro NC abgedeckt werden.

Seitenstruktur

S.1 Audit Details + Terminology S.2 Categorization of Findings S.3 Client Response Deadlines (All Schemes + MDSAP) S.4 Audit Findings Acknowledgement S.5+ NC Finding Cards (wiederholt je NC) Ende Conclusion + Recommendation + Signature Ende Version History

NC Card Struktur (gemäß Vorlage ID 2413)

NC Header

  • NC Number + NC Type (Minor/Major) + MDSAP Grade (1–5)

Audit Team: Description of Detected NC

  • 1. Requirement (ALLE Schemes: ISO 13485, MDR, MDSAP)
  • 2. Description of nonconformity
  • 3. Supporting objective evidence
  • 4. Context and Significance (required for MDSAP)
  • 5. Identification of associated product(s)

Client Response

  • 1. Root cause analysis
  • 2. Root cause(s)
  • 3. Correction(s)
  • 4. Corrective and/or preventive action(s)
  • 5. Completion due date

TÜV SÜD Review (Prozessreihenfolge!)

  • 1. Client response receiving date
  • 2. TÜV SÜD acceptance of client response
  • 3. TÜV SÜD evidence of implementation reviewed
  • 4. TÜV SÜD acceptance of implementation evidence
  • 5. TÜV SÜD evidence of effectiveness reviewed
  • 6. TÜV SÜD acceptance of effectiveness
3 Status-Optionen: Für alle Acceptance-Felder müssen immer drei Optionen verfügbar sein: OK, Not OK, To be verified. "To be verified during upcoming audit" ist häufigster Fall.
Alle Schemes: Jede NC muss immer gegen ALLE auditierten regulatorischen Anforderungen geschrieben werden (ISO 13485, MDR, MDSAP). Wenn ein Scheme nicht applicable ist, muss "N/A" stehen.

Terminology
Requirement: Need or expectation that is stated, generally implied or obligatory such as: Any part of a law, ordinance, decree, or other regulation that applies to the Quality Management System of an organization involved in one or more stages of the life cycle of a medical device (e.g., (EN) ISO 13485, MDR, IVDR, MDSAP, MDD, AIMDD, IVD, (DIN EN) ISO 9001 etc.). Defined processes and documentation of the auditee's management system. TÜV SÜD terms and conditions.

Objective evidence: Data supporting the existence or verity of something such as records, statements of fact or other information. This evidence is relevant to the audit criteria and is verifiable.

Nonconformity: The non-fulfillment of a requirement.

Categorization of Findings
Major Nonconformity: Non-fulfillment of a requirement that affects the capability of the management system to achieve the intended results. Nonconformities could be classified as major in the following circumstances:
– If there is a significant doubt that effective process control is in place, or that products or services will meet specified requirements.
– A number of minor nonconformities associated with the same requirement or issue could demonstrate a systemic failure and thus constitute a major nonconformity.

Minor Nonconformity: Non-fulfillment of a requirement that does not affect the capability of the management system to achieve the intended results.

Grade 1 – 5 (Mandatory for MDSAP): Grade of each finding according to GHTF/SG3/N19:2012 "Quality management system – Medical devices – Nonconformity Grading System for Regulatory Purposes and Information Exchange" and MDSAP AU P0037 "Guidelines on the use of GHTF/SG3/N19:2012 for MDSAP purposes".

Note: Improvement Potential (I) is not allowed. For combined audits (multi-scheme), the stricter timelines apply. For multi-sites, consider IAF MD1 requirement to determine whether systemic deficiencies apply to other sites.

Audit Findings Acknowledgement
When a nonconformity is found, a record of this form should be completed even if the auditee corrects the nonconformity during the audit. In such case, a record of the auditee's actions to resolve the issue should be described.

This document contains all audit findings. It shall be issued, through email, immediately after (before or during) the onsite audit at the closing meeting. The findings are classified as described above. Nonconformities shall be presented during the closing meeting in such a manner that they are understood by the client and the time frame for responding shall be agreed. This is acknowledged by the client by signing the "Receipt of Audit" section of the MHS Opening and Closing Document Package (ID 163350) and MHS UAA Opening and Closing Document Package (ID 332606) in case of unannounced audit.

(Note: "Understood" does not necessarily mean that the nonconformity has been accepted by the client.)

Failure to respond adequately by the due date may result in suspension or withdrawal of certification.
