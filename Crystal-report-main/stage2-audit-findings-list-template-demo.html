Nonconformities
NC Number: NC-2025-001 Minor Grade: 2
Audit Team: Description of Detected Nonconformity
Requirement
ISO 13485: Clause 8.5.2 – Corrective Action: The organization shall document a procedure to define requirements for reviewing nonconformities, determining the causes of nonconformities, and evaluating the need for action.
MDR: Annex IX, Section 2.3 (d) – The quality management system shall address corrective and preventive action and verification of the effectiveness of any corrective action taken.
MDSAP: MDSAP AU P0015.006 Task 3 – Measurement, Analysis and Improvement – CAPA process.
Description of nonconformity
The CAPA procedure (SOP-QA-042, Rev. 7) does not include a defined timeline for the completion of root cause analysis following identification of a nonconformity. Review of 3 out of 8 sampled CAPAs (CAPA-2024-017, CAPA-2024-023, CAPA-2024-031) showed root cause analyses completed between 45 and 78 days after initial identification, without documented justification for the extended duration.
Supporting objective evidence
– SOP-QA-042 Rev. 7, effective 2024-01-15: No defined timeline for root cause analysis.
– CAPA-2024-017: NC identified 2024-02-10, RCA completed 2024-04-28 (78 days).
– CAPA-2024-023: NC identified 2024-05-03, RCA completed 2024-06-17 (45 days).
– CAPA-2024-031: NC identified 2024-08-20, RCA completed 2024-10-15 (56 days).
Context and Significance (required for MDSAP)
Absence of a defined timeline may lead to delayed identification and elimination of systemic quality issues. While CAPAs were eventually completed, extended timelines reduce effectiveness. No product safety issues identified as a direct result.
Identification of associated product(s)
CardioFlow™ Vascular Catheter System (REF MT-CF-200), NeuroSense™ EEG Monitor (REF MT-NS-500)
Client Response: Root Cause Analysis, Root Cause, Correction, and Corrective Action
Root cause analysis
5-Why analysis: SOP drafted in 2019 with fewer CAPAs, informal timeline expectations not updated as volume increased.
Root cause(s)
SOP-QA-042 not updated with specific RCA timelines. Management review tracked only overall CAPA closure, not milestone metrics.
Correction(s)
Immediate review of 4 open CAPAs to ensure RCA completion within 14 days. Two outstanding RCAs completed 2025-04-05.
Corrective and/or preventive action(s)
1. SOP-QA-042 Rev. 8: max 30 days for RCA, escalation to Quality Director if exceeded.
2. CAPA dashboard updated with "RCA Due" milestone + auto-reminders.
3. Management Review inputs updated to include milestone metrics.
Completion due date
2025-05-30

NC Number: NC-2025-002 Major Grade: 4
Audit Team: Description of Detected Nonconformity
Requirement
ISO 13485: Clause 7.5.6 – Validation of processes for production and service provision where resulting output cannot be verified by subsequent monitoring or measurement.
MDR: Annex IX, Section 2.3 (a) – The QMS shall address production processes and their verification, in particular validation of special processes.
MDSAP: MDSAP AU P0013.006 Task 6 – Production and Service Controls: Verify validation of production processes.
Description of nonconformity
The ultrasonic welding process (PRD-UW-003) for CardioFlow™ catheter hub-to-shaft connection was not revalidated after equipment change (Horn Assembly HA-200 → HA-300 on 2024-07-15). Original validation PV-2022-014 was performed with HA-200 (20 kHz, conical tip). HA-300 operates at 35 kHz with stepped tip geometry. Change Request CR-2024-089 classified this as "minor" and did not trigger revalidation.
Supporting objective evidence
– PV-2022-014: Validated with HA-200 (20 kHz, conical tip).
– CR-2024-089: Classified as "minor change", approved 2024-07-10.
– HA-300 specs: 35 kHz, stepped tip – differs from validated parameters.
– Lots CF-2024-0801 through CF-2025-0312: Produced with non-revalidated process.
– SOP-PRD-015 Rev. 5, Section 4.3.2: Requires revalidation for equipment changes, but SOP-QA-030 does not cross-reference this.
Context and Significance (required for MDSAP)
Ultrasonic welding is a special process – bond strength cannot be fully verified non-destructively. Failure to revalidate after equipment change creates risk that products since 2024-07-15 may not meet specifications. Inadequate hub-to-shaft integrity could result in device separation during clinical use. Systemic gap between change management and process validation procedures.
Identification of associated product(s)
CardioFlow™ Vascular Catheter System (REF MT-CF-200), all sizes. Lots CF-2024-0801 through CF-2025-0312.
Client Response: Root Cause Analysis, Root Cause, Correction, and Corrective Action
Root cause analysis
Fishbone (6M) analysis: SOP-QA-030 classifies changes by equipment "type" not by impact on validated parameters. Horn replacement seen as "like-for-like" (same manufacturer). No cross-reference to process validation records.
Root cause(s)
1. SOP-QA-030 classification matrix lacks assessment of impact on validated process parameters.
2. No procedural link between change management and process validation to trigger revalidation assessment.
Correction(s)
1. Process revalidation (IQ/OQ/PQ) for PRD-UW-003 with HA-300 initiated 2025-04-02.
2. Retrospective risk assessment of affected lots using retained samples – preliminary results: bond strength within specification.
Corrective and/or preventive action(s)
1. SOP-QA-030 revision: mandatory cross-reference to PV records for equipment changes on special processes.
2. Linkage table mapping special processes → validated parameters → equipment.
3. Training of Production Engineering and QA staff.
4. Preventive review of all other special processes (n=6).
Completion due date
2025-05-15 (revalidation) / 2025-06-15 (procedure + training)
