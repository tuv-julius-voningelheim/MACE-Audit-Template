Categorization of Findings
Major Nonconformity: Non-fulfillment of a requirement that affects the capability of the management system to achieve the intended results. Nonconformities could be classified as major in the following circumstances:
– If there is a significant doubt that effective process control is in place, or that products or services will meet specified requirements.
– A number of minor nonconformities associated with the same requirement or issue could demonstrate a systemic failure and thus constitute a major nonconformity.

Minor Nonconformity: Non-fulfillment of a requirement that does not affect the capability of the management system to achieve the intended results.

Grade 1 – 5 (Mandatory for MDSAP): Grade of each finding according to GHTF/SG3/N19:2012 "Quality management system – Medical devices – Nonconformity Grading System for Regulatory Purposes and Information Exchange" and MDSAP AU P0037.

Note: Improvement Potential (I) is not allowed. For combined audits (multi-scheme), the stricter timelines apply.

Nonconformities
NC Number: 1 Minor Grade: 2
Audit Team: Description of Detected Nonconformity
Requirement
ISO 13485: Clause 8.5.2 – Corrective Action: The organization shall take action to eliminate the cause of nonconformities in order to prevent recurrence.
MDR: Annex IX, Chapter I, Section 2.2 (d) – The quality management system shall address corrective and preventive actions.
MDSAP: Task 7 – Measurement, Analysis, and Improvement – CAPA System
Description of nonconformity
The CAPA system does not adequately ensure that root cause analyses are completed systematically. In 3 of 8 sampled CAPA records (CAPA-2024-047, CAPA-2024-062, CAPA-2025-011), the root cause analysis was incomplete or missing, and corrective actions did not address the identified root cause.
Supporting objective evidence
CAPA-2024-047: Root cause documented as "operator error" without further analysis. No 5-Why or Fishbone methodology applied. CAPA-2024-062: Root cause field left blank; corrective action defined as "retraining" without evidence of root cause determination. CAPA-2025-011: Root cause analysis references a document (RCA-2025-003) that does not exist in the QMS.
Context and Significance (required for MDSAP)
The CAPA system is a fundamental element of the quality management system. Ineffective root cause analysis may lead to recurring nonconformities and potentially affect product safety in case corrective actions do not address the actual cause of identified problems.
Identification of associated product(s) (if applicable)
CardioMonitor Pro (CM-500), SurgAssist Retractor System (SA-200)
Client Response: Root Cause Analysis, Root Cause, Correction, and Corrective Action
Root cause analysis
5-Why analysis performed: Why 1 – CAPAs were closed without complete RCA? Because there was no mandatory review step before closure. Why 2 – Why no mandatory review step? Because the CAPA procedure (SOP-QA-042) does not define specific RCA completion criteria. Why 3 – Why no specific criteria? Because the SOP was last revised in 2019 and did not incorporate updated expectations.
Root cause(s)
CAPA procedure SOP-QA-042 Rev.5 lacks defined criteria for root cause analysis completeness and does not require independent review of RCA adequacy before CAPA closure.
Correction(s) to eliminate detected nonconformity
The three identified CAPAs (CAPA-2024-047, -062, -2025-011) have been reopened and root cause analyses have been completed using the 5-Why methodology. Completed: 2025-11-01.
Corrective and/or preventive action(s)
1. SOP-QA-042 will be revised to include mandatory RCA completeness criteria and independent review requirement. Target: 2025-12-15.
2. Training on updated RCA requirements for all QA personnel. Target: 2026-01-15.
3. Retrospective review of all open CAPAs (n=12) for RCA adequacy. Target: 2026-01-31.
Completion due date
2026-01-31

NC Number: 2 Major Grade: 4
Audit Team: Description of Detected Nonconformity
Requirement
ISO 13485: Clause 7.3.4 – Design and development review: At suitable stages, systematic reviews of design and development shall be performed.
MDR: Annex IX, Chapter II, Section 4.4 – The notified body shall verify that the manufacturer has applied the provisions of Annex II.
MDSAP: Task 5 – Design and Development – Design Reviews and Design Verification
Description of nonconformity
Design reviews for the CardioMonitor Pro V2.0 (project PRJ-2024-089) were not performed at suitable stages as required. The design review between design input and design output phases was skipped. The design review meeting minutes (DRM-2024-089-03) show that critical design inputs related to biocompatibility and electrical safety were not reviewed prior to design verification activities.
Supporting objective evidence
Design History File for PRJ-2024-089 reviewed. DRM-2024-089-01 (Concept Review): completed 2024-03-15. DRM-2024-089-02 (Design Input Review): scheduled for 2024-06-01, marked as "postponed – to be combined with next review". DRM-2024-089-03 (Design Output Review): completed 2024-09-20 – meeting minutes do not reference design inputs DI-089-007 (biocompatibility) and DI-089-012 (electrical safety per IEC 60601-1). Design verification protocol DVP-089-001 was executed on 2024-08-15, before the design output review.
Context and Significance (required for MDSAP)
Skipping design review stages for a Class IIb cardiac monitoring device represents a significant gap in the design control process. Unreviewed design inputs related to biocompatibility and electrical safety could result in devices that do not meet essential safety requirements, posing a direct risk to patient safety.
Identification of associated product(s)
CardioMonitor Pro V2.0 (CM-500-V2), Class IIb, MDR Certificate pending
Client Response: Root Cause Analysis, Root Cause, Correction, and Corrective Action
Root cause analysis
Fishbone analysis conducted. Organizational cause: R&D department experienced resource constraints in Q2 2024 due to parallel product launch. Process cause: Design review procedure SOP-RD-015 allows design reviews to be "combined" with subsequent reviews without defining criteria or requiring management approval. The procedure does not enforce a mandatory hold point before design verification.
Root cause(s)
1. SOP-RD-015 Rev.3 does not define mandatory design review stages that cannot be skipped or combined. 2. No procedural requirement for management approval when deviating from the design review plan. 3. No hold point mechanism to prevent design verification before completion of required design reviews.
Correction(s) to eliminate detected nonconformity
1. Retrospective design review DRM-2024-089-02R conducted on 2025-11-05 covering all outstanding design inputs incl. DI-089-007 and DI-089-012. 2. Impact assessment performed: design verification results DVP-089-001 reviewed against now-complete design review outputs. No gaps identified that affect product safety. Documentation updated in Design History File.
Corrective and/or preventive action(s)
1. SOP-RD-015 will be revised to define mandatory design review stages as hold points. Target: 2025-12-31.
2. Design review plan template updated to include mandatory checkpoints. Target: 2025-12-15.
3. Training for all R&D project leads on updated procedure. Target: 2026-01-31.
4. Preventive: All active design projects (n=4) audited for design review completeness. Target: 2025-12-31.
Completion due date
2026-01-31
